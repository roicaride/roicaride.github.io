Electric Scooter Project

From Concept to Reality: A Journey of Innovation and Learning

A hands-on journey into electric vehicle design and engineering

The Journey

What started as a personal challenge turned into an incredible learning experience. Despite having no prior experience in mechanical engineering or electric vehicle design, I embarked on the journey of building an electric scooter from scratch, starting with a children's scooter frame. This project became a testament to the power of self-directed learning and hands-on engineering.

The process was both challenging and incredibly rewarding. Coming from a business and technology background, I had to learn everything from scratch - from understanding electric motor specifications to mastering welding techniques. Each obstacle became an opportunity to learn something new, and the satisfaction of solving these challenges was immense.

Technical Specifications

Power System

  • 48V 15Ah Battery Pack
  • 1500W Rear Wheel Motor
  • Custom Battery Management System
  • High-efficiency Controller

Mechanical Components

  • Custom Solidworks-designed Chassis
  • 16" Front Wheel (Bicycle)
  • 12" Rear Wheel with Traction
  • Shimano MTB Hydraulic Brakes
  • Reinforced Frame Structure

Custom Features

  • Foldable BMX Handlebar
  • Custom Central Battery Box
  • Custom Brake Adaptors
  • LED Lighting System
  • Digital Speed Display

Design & Manufacturing Process

The project began with extensive research and planning. Despite my lack of experience in mechanical engineering, I dove headfirst into learning CAD design using Solidworks. The learning curve was steep, but incredibly rewarding. Each component was carefully designed and tested virtually before being manufactured.

The manufacturing process was a journey in itself:

  • Learning CAD modeling from scratch to design custom parts
  • Mastering welding techniques for structural integrity
  • Understanding electrical systems and safety protocols
  • Creating custom components with limited tools
  • Rigorous safety testing and optimization

What made this project special was the combination of technical learning and practical problem-solving. Every day brought new challenges, and each solution taught me something valuable about engineering and design.

Challenges & Solutions

As someone with no prior experience in mechanical engineering, I faced numerous challenges that required innovative solutions:

  • Learning to design a stable frame that could handle the increased power output
  • Understanding and implementing proper electrical safety measures
  • Creating custom adaptors for the braking system without professional tools
  • Optimizing weight distribution for better handling and stability
  • Managing battery placement and protection from environmental factors

Each challenge was an opportunity to learn and grow. The satisfaction of overcoming these obstacles was immense, and the knowledge gained was invaluable.

Results & Performance

The final product exceeded all expectations. Not only did it perform better than anticipated, but the learning experience was transformative. The scooter achieves impressive speeds while maintaining stability and safety, a testament to the careful planning and execution of the project.

What started as a personal challenge became a demonstration of what's possible with determination and a willingness to learn. The custom design elements not only enhance functionality but also give the scooter a unique, professional appearance that reflects the care and attention to detail put into every aspect of the project.

This project reinforced several important principles that extend beyond engineering:
